1.1 Guiding Principles

  • “Simple things should be simple; complex things should be possible.”

    – Alan Kay, pioneering computer scientist

  • Provide a flexible tool for thinking humans.

  • Let the user make their own decisions about how to best organize their information.

  • Allow the user to see what’s going on under the hood.

  • Tinkering is not only allowed, but encouraged.

  • Your information should always be easily accessible, and you should be able to access your data using a variety of widely available tools.
